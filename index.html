Who I Am

Scientist. Explorer.
Naturalist at heart.

I am a Metabolomics and Lipidomics Senior Research Scientist and Manager at the Life Sciences Centre, University of British Columbia, Vancouver. My work sits at the intersection of analytical chemistry, mass spectrometry, and the rich molecular world of mammalian biology and natural products.

Holding a Summa Cum Laude PhD in Analytical Chemistry from Guanajuato University (Mexico), and having collaborated across three continents — Australia, Japan, and North America — I bring a global perspective to cutting-edge science. My postdoctoral work at the Linus Pauling Institute focused on metabolomics and lipidomics pathway analysis, biomarker discovery, and understanding how dietary molecules shape brain and metabolic resilience in mammalian systems.

Beyond the lab, I find the same wonder in nature's complexity whether I'm looking through a mass spectrometer or hiking through old-growth forests. The mountains and the metabolome are both endlessly intricate — and endlessly worth exploring.

Scientific Focus

What I Study

My research develops and applies advanced LC-HRMS/MS platforms to decode the lipidome and metabolome of mammalian biological systems — connecting molecular-level chemistry to human health outcomes, with a particular focus on cardiovascular and neurological disease.

🧬

Metabolomics & Lipidomics

Co-equal pillars of my research. Developing advanced LC-HRMS/MS workflows to comprehensively profile the small-molecule and lipid landscapes of mammalian tissues, biofluids, and cell models to identify disease biomarkers.

🫀

Cardiovascular & Lipid Biology

Investigating lipid and oxylipin profiles in heart failure and coronary artery disease — using lipidomics of cardiac tissue and plasma to discover prognostic biomarkers and understand disease mechanisms in human samples.

🧠

Neurological Resilience

Exploring how botanical metabolites and lipid mediators protect the aging mammalian brain. Leveraging metabolomics and lipidomics of brain tissue and plasma to understand neuroprotective mechanisms.

🐭

Mammalian & Mouse Models

Applying standardized MS-based metabolomics and lipidomics to characterize mouse models of aging, frailty, and metabolic disease — bridging preclinical findings to human biology.

🍄

Microbial & Host Metabolomics

Using comparative metabolomics to probe host-microbe metabolic interactions — from fungal pathogens like Cryptococcus neoformans to honey bee gut symbionts — revealing how microbes shape host physiology.

⚗️

Method Development & Core Science

Building robust analytical workflows for clinical metabolomics and lipidomics, including methods for OCT-embedded biobanked tissues, global lipid extraction optimization, and nucleic acid methylation profiling.
